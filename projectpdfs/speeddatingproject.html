Introduction¶

Background¶

Before dating apps became all the rage, there was this thing called speed dating that was meant to be a fun way to cut down on the mountains of time individuals spent trying to find a partner. Speed dating was a solution that allowed one to meet and interact with a large number of potential partners in a relatively short amount of time -- often just 4 minutes, as in this experiment. In this report, I want to dissect what people are looking for in their speed dating matches, if there exist gender differences (which will be limited to male and female in this dataset), and other factors (such as the order in which people met potential partners) that might influence peoples' decisions.

Data¶

The dataset we will explore was drawn from "Speed Dating Experiment", found on Kaggle.com. Through a series of speed dating experiments conducted from 2002-2004 by Columbia Business School professors Ray Fisman and Sheena Iyengar for their paper Gender Differences in Mate Selection: Evidence From a Speed Dating Experiment, a dataset compiling demographics, dating habits, lifestyle information, and various attributes was collected. When participants signed up, they filled out a baseline survey. Then participants rated each other after each 4-minute interaction. Finally, individuals were asked if they would like a second date with any of the potential partners they met during the experiment, and rated them again on similar questions as their own baseline survey. Note that these experiments collected information from heterosexual male-identifying and heterosexual female-identifying individuals.

For more analysis from Iyengar and Fisman, read Racial Preferences in Dating.

  • 552 participants in the experiment from 2002-2004
  • Dates were 4 minutes long
  • Survey questions included in dataset:
    • If they would like to see their date again
    • Attractiveness
    • Sincerity
    • Intelligence
    • Fun
    • Ambition
    • Shared Interests
  • Participant information collected:
    • Demoraphics
    • Dating habits
    • Self-perceptions across key attributes
    • Beliefs on what others find valuable in a mate
    • Lifestyle information

Questions were asked in 3 stages:

  1. Before the date
  2. After the date
  3. 3-4 weeks after the date

Data Exploration Ideas

  • What are the least desirable attributes in a male partner?
  • Does this differ for female partners?
  • How important do people think attractiveness is in potential mate selection vs. its real impact?
  • Are shared interests more important than a shared racial background?
  • Can people accurately predict their own perceived value in the dating market?
  • In terms of getting a second date, is it better to be someone's first speed date of the night or their last?

# creates dataset of only rated characteristics
def perceptionanalysis(dataset):
    attractiveonly = pd.DataFrame(dataset.loc[:, "AvgAttractive1"])
    attractiveonly = attractiveonly.rename(columns = {"AvgAttractive1": "Score"})
    attractiveonly["Type of Attribute"] = ["Attractive", "Attractive"]
    
    sincereonly = pd.DataFrame(dataset.loc[:, "AvgSincere1"])
    sincereonly = sincereonly.rename(columns = {"AvgSincere1": "Score"})
    sincereonly["Type of Attribute"] = ["Sincere", "Sincere"]

    funonly = pd.DataFrame(dataset.loc[:, "AvgFun1"])
    funonly = funonly.rename(columns = {"AvgFun1": "Score"})
    funonly["Type of Attribute"] = ["Fun", "Fun"]
    
    ambonly = pd.DataFrame(dataset.loc[:, "AvgAmb1"])
    ambonly = ambonly.rename(columns = {"AvgAmb1": "Score"})
    ambonly["Type of Attribute"] = ["Ambitious", "Ambitious"]
    
    sharonly = pd.DataFrame(dataset.loc[:, "AvgShar1"])
    sharonly = sharonly.rename(columns = {"AvgShar1": "Score"})
    sharonly["Type of Attribute"] = ["Shared Interests", "Shared Interests"]
    
    intelonly = pd.DataFrame(dataset.loc[:, "AvgIntel1"])
    intelonly = intelonly.rename(columns = {"AvgIntel1": "Score"})
    intelonly["Type of Attribute"] = ["Intelligent", "Intelligent"]
    
    return pd.concat([attractiveonly, sincereonly, funonly, ambonly, sharonly, intelonly])

Conclusion¶

This data exploration didn't really give us any better answers to the secrets of love, or at least mastering the first date. It's not shared interests or hobbies, it's not attractiveness or intelligence or other commonly thought to be important traits. It's just how much someone likes you, which we see reflected in the variables dec_o and dec.

To summarize, we learned:

  • Both genders represented in this study like dinner + a movie
  • Women are more selective
  • A match happens more often than unrequited love
  • Attractiveness does not factor into rejection
  • Your impression of yourself is not what other people think of you
  • There is no key secret to being likeable in any of the traits measured in this study
  • Being likeable is the most important component of securing a second date
